# Article Writing Guide

## Article Types

### How-To / Tutorial
- **Purpose:** Teach the reader to accomplish something specific
- **Structure:** Problem → Prerequisites → Steps → Result → Troubleshooting
- **Tip:** Number your steps, include expected output at each stage

### Comparison
- **Purpose:** Help the reader choose between options
- **Structure:** Criteria → Option A → Option B → Verdict → "Best for X"
- **Tip:** Be honest about trade-offs — biased comparisons lose trust

### Thought Leadership
- **Purpose:** Share a perspective or insight that shifts thinking
- **Structure:** Contrarian hook → Evidence → Implications → Call to action
- **Tip:** Take a clear stance — hedged opinions don't get shared

### Case Study
- **Purpose:** Show real results from a real situation
- **Structure:** Challenge → Approach → Results → Lessons
- **Tip:** Lead with the outcome number, then tell the story

### Announcement / Release
- **Purpose:** Communicate what's new and why it matters
- **Structure:** What changed → Why it matters → How to use it → What's next
- **Tip:** Users care about their benefit, not your effort

## Article Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Title is specific and promises a clear benefit
- [ ] First paragraph hooks the reader (no throat-clearing)
- [ ] Subheadings work as a standalone outline
- [ ] Every section earns its place (cut anything that doesn't add value)
- [ ] At least one concrete example per major point
- [ ] Internal links to related content
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources where appropriate
- [ ] Meta description written (150-160 characters)
- [ ] Featured image or hero visual selected
- [ ] CTA is clear and relevant to the content
- [ ] Proofread for typos, grammar, and tone consistency
- [ ] Tested on mobile (formatting, image sizing, readability)
