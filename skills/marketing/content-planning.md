# Content Planning

## Content Pillars

Define 3-5 core topics your content revolves around. Every article and post should map to a pillar.

### How to Choose Pillars
1. **What does your product do?** — Core functionality and use cases
2. **What does your audience care about?** — Pain points, aspirations, industry trends
3. **Where do those overlap?** — The intersection is your content territory

### Example Pillar Structure
```
Pillar 1: [Core product domain]
  → Articles: tutorials, how-tos, best practices
  → Social: tips, quick wins, before/after

Pillar 2: [Adjacent problem your audience has]
  → Articles: guides, comparisons, frameworks
  → Social: insights, polls, discussions

Pillar 3: [Industry/community perspective]
  → Articles: thought leadership, case studies, trends
  → Social: opinions, news commentary, community highlights
```

## Publishing Schedule

### Blog
- Set a cadence you can sustain (weekly, biweekly, monthly)
- Batch creation: research → outline → draft → edit → publish
- Maintain a backlog of 3-5 article ideas at all times

### Social Media
- Define per-platform frequency (e.g., Twitter 3x/week, LinkedIn 2x/week)
- Mix content types: original posts, article shares, engagement posts
- Use scheduling tools — don't rely on real-time posting

## Content Repurposing

One piece of content should feed multiple channels:

```
Blog article
  → Twitter thread (key points)
  → LinkedIn post (professional angle)
  → Email newsletter snippet
  → Instagram carousel (visual summary)
```

## Topic Backlog

Maintain a simple list of content ideas with:
- **Topic** — What it's about
- **Pillar** — Which pillar it maps to
- **Type** — Article, social post, email, etc.
- **Priority** — High / Medium / Low
- **Status** — Idea / Outlined / Drafted / Published

## Review Cycle

### Monthly
- Review published content performance
- Identify top performers — what made them work?
- Identify underperformers — update, merge, or retire
- Replenish the topic backlog

### Quarterly
- Reassess content pillars — are they still aligned with product and audience?
- Audit existing content for accuracy and freshness
- Plan seasonal or event-based content
