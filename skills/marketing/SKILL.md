## Article Writing — Dos and Don'ts

### Do
- **Lead with value** — Open with the insight, not the backstory
- **Match search intent** — If people search "how to X", write a how-to, not an opinion piece
- **Use concrete examples** — Code snippets, screenshots, real numbers
- **Structure for scanning** — Subheadings, short paragraphs, bullet points
- **Include a clear takeaway** — What should the reader do after reading?
- **Link internally** — Connect related articles to build topic authority
- **Add a meta description** — 150-160 characters summarizing the article for search results

### Don't
- **Don't publish without a hook** — If the first paragraph doesn't grab attention, rewrite it
- **Don't write walls of text** — Break up every 2-3 sentences with formatting
- **Don't stuff keywords** — Write for humans; search engines follow
- **Don't skip the edit pass** — First drafts are never publish-ready
- **Don't ignore your existing content** — Update old posts before writing new ones on the same topic
- **Don't forget the CTA** — Every article should guide the reader somewhere next
- **Don't publish without proofreading** — Typos erode credibility

## Social Media Posts — Dos and Don'ts

### Do
- **Hook in the first line** — Social feeds are ruthless; earn the scroll-stop
- **One idea per post** — If you need a thread, break it into self-contained pieces
- **Use plain language** — Write like you're explaining to a smart friend
- **Include a visual when possible** — Images, diagrams, or code screenshots increase engagement
- **End with engagement** — Ask a question, invite a reply, prompt a share
- **Adapt tone to platform** — LinkedIn is professional, Twitter/X is conversational, Instagram is visual
- **Post consistently** — Regular cadence beats sporadic bursts

### Don't
- **Don't be overly promotional** — Ratio: 80% value, 20% promotion
- **Don't cross-post identically** — Each platform has its own format and expectations
- **Don't ignore replies** — Engagement is a conversation, not a broadcast
- **Don't use hashtag walls** — 2-5 relevant hashtags maximum
- **Don't post without context** — A link with no commentary gets scrolled past
- **Don't chase trends that don't fit** — Off-brand trend-jacking feels desperate
- **Don't forget the audience** — Write for your readers, not your peers

## Content Planning Basics

### Content Pillars
- Identify 3-5 core topics that align with your product and audience
- Every piece of content should map to a pillar
- Pillars keep content focused and build topical authority over time

### Publishing Cadence
- Set a realistic schedule you can maintain (weekly > daily-then-nothing)
- Quality over quantity — one strong piece beats three thin ones
- Repurpose across channels: article → social thread → email snippet

### Measurement
- Track what matters: traffic, engagement, conversions — not vanity metrics
- Review performance monthly and adjust pillars/topics accordingly
- Update underperforming content before creating new content on the same topic
