# Synthesis Patterns

Patterns for synthesizing multi-source research into actionable insights.

## Cross-Source Triangulation

Validate findings by seeking independent confirmation from multiple sources.

1. **Identify a claim** from one source
2. **Search for corroboration** in at least two other independent sources
3. **Assess independence** — sources citing the same original don't count as independent
4. **Rate confidence**:
   - **High**: 3+ independent sources agree, methodology is sound
   - **Medium**: 2 sources agree, or strong single source with sound methodology
   - **Low**: Single source, or sources with potential bias alignment

## Gap Analysis

Map the boundary between what is known and unknown.

1. **List the questions** the research should answer
2. **For each question**, catalog what evidence exists
3. **Identify gaps**: questions with no evidence, thin evidence, or outdated evidence
4. **Prioritize gaps** by impact on the decision being informed
5. **Recommend follow-up** research for high-priority gaps

## Contradiction Mapping

When sources disagree, diagnose why rather than picking a side.

1. **State the contradiction** clearly — what does each source claim?
2. **Compare methodology** — different methods often explain different conclusions
3. **Check context** — are sources discussing the same scope, time period, population?
4. **Assess credibility** — which source has stronger evidence, methodology, authority?
5. **Present both sides** with your assessment of relative strength

## Confidence Levels

Assign confidence to every major finding:

| Level | Criteria | Label |
|-------|----------|-------|
| High | Multiple independent sources, strong methodology, recent data | "Strong evidence suggests..." |
| Medium | Limited sources but credible, or older but likely still valid | "Evidence indicates..." |
| Low | Single source, weak methodology, or contradicted by others | "Preliminary evidence suggests..." |
| Speculative | No direct evidence; extrapolated from adjacent findings | "It is possible that..." |

## Evidence Weighting

When synthesizing across sources of varying quality:

- **Peer-reviewed research** > industry reports > blog posts > anecdotal accounts
- **Quantitative data** > qualitative observations (for measurable claims)
- **Recent sources** > older sources (for technology and market claims)
- **Primary sources** > secondary sources > tertiary sources
- **Methodology-transparent sources** > opaque sources
