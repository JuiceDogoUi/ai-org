# Content Strategy

## Article Structure

1. **Hook** — Open with a problem, question, or surprising fact
2. **Context** — Why this matters to the reader right now
3. **Body** — Deliver value with clear sections and examples
4. **Conclusion** — Summarize key takeaways, include CTA

## Writing Style

- Write at an 8th-grade reading level for broad accessibility
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Use subheadings every 200-300 words for scannability
- Include one concrete example per major point
- Avoid jargon without explanation
- Active voice over passive voice
- Front-load important information

## Content Types

### Tutorial
- Clear prerequisite and outcome
- Step-by-step with code examples
- Explain the "why" not just the "what"
- Include troubleshooting section

### Explainer
- Define the concept early
- Use analogies for complex ideas
- Compare and contrast with related concepts
- Link to deeper resources

### Case Study
- Problem → Solution → Results structure
- Specific numbers and outcomes
- Quote from customer/stakeholder
- Lessons learned

### Comparison
- Clear criteria for comparison
- Fair representation of all options
- Recommendation with reasoning
- "Best for X" conclusions

## SEO Fundamentals

### Keywords
- Primary keyword in title, first paragraph, and 1-2 subheadings
- Secondary keywords naturally throughout
- Long-tail variations for depth
- Search intent match (informational vs. transactional)

### On-Page Elements
- Title tag: Under 60 characters, keyword near front
- Meta description: Under 160 characters, includes keyword, compelling
- URL slug: Short, descriptive, includes keyword
- Image alt text: Descriptive, keyword-relevant

### Structure
- H1 for title (one per page)
- H2 for major sections
- H3 for subsections
- Internal links to 2-3 related articles
- External links to 1-2 authoritative sources

### Technical
- Page load speed matters for ranking
- Mobile-friendly layout
- Schema markup for rich results (FAQ, HowTo, Article)

## Editorial Calendar

### Planning
- Content pillars aligned to business goals
- Seasonal and event-based opportunities
- Competitor gap analysis
- Keyword research feeding topics

### Cadence
- Consistent publishing schedule builds audience expectations
- Quality over quantity
- Repurpose: article → social → email → video

### Review Process
- Draft → Technical review → Editorial review → Publish
- Style guide compliance check
- SEO checklist before publish
- Post-publish promotion plan

## Content Measurement

### Engagement Metrics
- Time on page (are they reading?)
- Scroll depth (how far?)
- Social shares
- Comments

### Business Metrics
- Organic traffic
- Keyword rankings
- Conversion rate from content
- Email signups from content

### Improvement Cycle
- Review top and bottom performers monthly
- Update underperforming content
- Expand successful content into series
- Retire or consolidate low-value content

## Avoid

- **Keyword stuffing** — Write for humans first, search engines second
- **Thin content** — If it doesn't provide unique value, don't publish
- **Clickbait headlines** — Deliver on what you promise
- **Walls of text** — Break up with formatting, images, examples
- **Outdated content** — Review and update regularly
- **Ignoring search intent** — Match content format to what users want
