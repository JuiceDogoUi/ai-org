# Regulatory Landscape

A reference map of major regulations by concern area. This is a starting point for identifying what to research — always verify current requirements from the official sources listed.

**Important**: This is not exhaustive. New regulations are enacted regularly. Always research whether additional regulations apply to the project's specific jurisdictions and industry.

## Privacy Regulations

| Regulation | Jurisdiction | Applies When | Official Source |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| GDPR | EU/EEA | Processing personal data of EU residents | gdpr.eu, ec.europa.eu |
| CCPA/CPRA | California, US | Businesses serving California residents above revenue/data thresholds | oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa |
| PIPEDA | Canada | Commercial processing of personal data in Canada | priv.gc.ca |
| LGPD | Brazil | Processing personal data of individuals in Brazil | gov.br/lgpd |
| APPI | Japan | Handling personal information in Japan | ppc.go.jp |
| POPIA | South Africa | Processing personal information in South Africa | justice.gov.za/popia |
| PDPA | Thailand | Processing personal data in Thailand | pdpc.or.th |
| PDPA | Singapore | Processing personal data in Singapore | pdpc.gov.sg |
| Privacy Act | Australia | Organizations with annual turnover above AUD 3M | oaic.gov.au |
| DPDPA | India | Processing digital personal data in India | meity.gov.in |

### Key differences to research per regulation:
- Definition of "personal data" (varies significantly)
- Lawful bases for processing (consent-only vs. legitimate interest)
- Data subject rights (which rights, response timelines)
- Cross-border transfer mechanisms
- Breach notification requirements (timelines, thresholds)
- Penalties and enforcement approach
- Age of consent for children's data

## Accessibility Standards

| Standard | Jurisdiction | Applies When | Official Source |
|---------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| WCAG 2.1/2.2 | International | Web content accessibility (most regulations reference this) | w3.org/WAI/WCAG22 |
| ADA Title III | US | Public accommodations (increasingly applied to websites) | ada.gov |
| Section 508 | US | Federal government IT | section508.gov |
| EAA | EU | Products and services in the EU market (from June 2025) | ec.europa.eu/social |
| EN 301 549 | EU | ICT products and services (references WCAG) | etsi.org |
| AODA | Ontario, Canada | Organizations in Ontario | ontario.ca/accessibility |

### Key differences to research:
- Which WCAG conformance level is required (A, AA, or AAA)
- Which WCAG version is referenced (2.0, 2.1, or 2.2)
- Scope (web only, or also mobile, desktop, documents)
- Enforcement mechanism and penalties
- Compliance timelines and exemptions

## How to Use This Map

1. **Identify project jurisdictions** — where are users, where is data stored, where does the business operate?
2. **Identify data types** — personal data, financial data, health data, children's data?
3. **Scan this table** for potentially applicable regulations
4. **Research each candidate** from its official source to confirm applicability and current requirements
5. **Document which regulations apply** in the audit scope section
