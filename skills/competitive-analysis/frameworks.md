# Analysis Frameworks

## Porter's Five Forces

Assess industry attractiveness and competitive intensity.

### 1. Threat of New Entrants
- **High barrier**: Regulated industries, high capital requirements, strong network effects
- **Low barrier**: SaaS, digital products, open-source alternatives
- **Assess**: How easily can a new competitor enter this space?

### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers
- **High power**: Few suppliers, switching costs, proprietary technology
- **Low power**: Many alternatives, commoditized inputs, easy switching
- **Assess**: Can our dependencies (APIs, infrastructure, data providers) squeeze us?

### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers
- **High power**: Few large customers, low switching costs, price-sensitive market
- **Low power**: Many small customers, high switching costs, differentiated product
- **Assess**: How easily can customers leave or negotiate down?

### 4. Threat of Substitutes
- **High threat**: Different approach solves the same problem (e.g., video calls vs. travel)
- **Low threat**: No viable alternative, deeply embedded in workflows
- **Assess**: What completely different solution could replace us?

### 5. Competitive Rivalry
- **Intense**: Many similar competitors, slow growth, low differentiation
- **Low**: Clear market leader, fast growth, strong differentiation
- **Assess**: How fierce is the fight for market share?

## SWOT Analysis

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---------|---------|
| **Internal** | **Strengths** -- What we do well, unique resources, advantages | **Weaknesses** -- Gaps, resource constraints, where we lag |
| **External** | **Opportunities** -- Market trends, unmet needs, partnerships | **Threats** -- Competitor moves, regulation, market shifts |

### Effective SWOT
- Be specific: "3x faster onboarding than Competitor X" not "good UX"
- Connect quadrants: use strengths to capture opportunities, address weaknesses that amplify threats
- Prioritize: rank items by impact and likelihood
- Action-oriented: each item should suggest a strategic move

## Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD)

Focus on what customers are trying to accomplish, not product features.

### Template
```text
When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].
```

### Example
```text
When I'm preparing a client presentation,
I want to quickly find relevant data visualizations,
so I can make a compelling case without spending hours on charts.
```

### Application to Competitive Analysis
- Map competitor features to the jobs they address
- Identify jobs that are over-served (too many competitors) vs. under-served (opportunity)
- Evaluate competitors on how well they fulfill the job, not feature count

## Value Chain Analysis

Map the activities that create value, identify where competitors invest.

### Primary Activities
1. **Inbound logistics** -- How inputs are acquired (data, content, code)
2. **Operations** -- How the product is built and maintained
3. **Outbound logistics** -- How value reaches customers (distribution, platform)
4. **Marketing & Sales** -- How customers are acquired
5. **Service** -- How customers are supported post-sale

### Support Activities
- **Infrastructure** -- Management, legal, finance
- **HR** -- Talent acquisition and retention
- **Technology** -- R&D, platform capabilities
- **Procurement** -- Vendor and partner management

### Application
- Map each competitor's value chain
- Identify where they invest most (indicates strategic priority)
- Find activities where you can achieve cost advantage or differentiation

## Competitive Response Matrix

Predict how competitors will react to your moves.

| Your Move | Competitor A (likely response) | Competitor B (likely response) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Price reduction | Match within 2 weeks (price-sensitive market) | Unlikely to match (premium positioning) |
| New feature X | Copy within 6 months (fast release cycle) | Ignore (different target segment) |
| Enter market Y | Defensive campaign (Y is their stronghold) | Partnership opportunity (complementary) |

### Factors Affecting Response
- **Speed**: How fast can they react? (org size, tech debt, decision-making)
- **Motivation**: How much do they care? (revenue impact, strategic importance)
- **Capability**: Can they actually respond? (technical ability, resources)
