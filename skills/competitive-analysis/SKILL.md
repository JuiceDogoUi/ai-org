# Competitive Analysis

## Research Framework

1. **Identify competitors** — Direct, indirect, and potential
2. **Gather data** — Features, pricing, positioning, reviews, market presence
3. **Analyze** — Strengths, weaknesses, gaps, opportunities
4. **Synthesize** — Strategic recommendations

## Competitor Types

- **Direct** — Same product category, same customer (e.g., Slack vs. Teams)
- **Indirect** — Different solution, same problem (e.g., Slack vs. email)
- **Potential** — Could enter your space (e.g., big tech, adjacent startups)

## Research Sources

### Primary
- Competitor product trials and demos
- Sales conversations (win/loss interviews)
- Customer interviews (why did they choose/leave?)
- Industry events and conferences

### Secondary
- Competitor websites and marketing materials
- Pricing pages and feature lists
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius reviews
- Press releases and news coverage
- Job postings (reveal priorities and tech stack)
- LinkedIn company pages and employee growth
- Crunchbase, PitchBook (funding, investors)
- Social media presence and engagement

## Analysis Dimensions

### Product
- Feature comparison (what they offer vs. what we offer)
- User experience quality
- Technical architecture (if visible)
- Integration ecosystem
- Roadmap signals (from job posts, announcements)

### Market Position
- Target audience and ICP
- Messaging and value proposition
- Brand perception
- Market share (if available)

### Business Model
- Pricing structure and tiers
- Free tier or trial availability
- Contract terms and commitment
- Upsell and expansion strategy

### Go-to-Market
- Sales motion (self-serve, sales-led, hybrid)
- Marketing channels and spend
- Content strategy
- Partnership ecosystem

### Strengths & Weaknesses
- What they do well (defend or differentiate)
- Where they fall short (opportunity)
- Customer complaints (from reviews)
- What customers praise

## Strategic Frameworks

### SWOT
- **Strengths** — Internal advantages
- **Weaknesses** — Internal disadvantages
- **Opportunities** — External factors to exploit
- **Threats** — External factors to mitigate

### Porter's Five Forces
- Threat of new entrants
- Bargaining power of suppliers
- Bargaining power of buyers
- Threat of substitutes
- Competitive rivalry

### Jobs to Be Done
- What job is the customer hiring the product to do?
- What are the functional, emotional, and social dimensions?
- Where do competitors over-serve or under-serve?

## Output Formats

### Comparison Matrix
| Feature | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---------|-----|--------------|--------------|
| Feature 1 | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ |

### Competitive Landscape Map
- Plot competitors on 2x2 matrix (e.g., price vs. complexity)
- Identify white space opportunities

### Battlecard
- Single-page sales enablement
- Key differentiators
- Common objections and responses
- Win themes

### SWOT Summary
- Per-competitor SWOT
- Aggregated market SWOT

## Ongoing Monitoring

- Set up Google Alerts for competitor names
- Monitor G2/Capterra for new reviews
- Track pricing page changes
- Follow competitor blogs and newsletters
- Review quarterly for updates

## Avoid

- **Confirmation bias** — Don't just look for evidence you're better
- **Outdated intelligence** — Refresh analysis quarterly at minimum
- **Feature obsession** — Positioning and GTM matter as much as features
- **Copying competitors** — Differentiate, don't imitate
- **Analysis paralysis** — Deliver actionable recommendations, not encyclopedias
- **Ignoring indirect competitors** — Often the biggest threat
